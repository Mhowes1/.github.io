Under the Shell began the second I stepped onto Maryland's campus. Even before walked into a class with my grey Under Armour backpack slung acorss my shoulders.

I met Sam Jane and Brendan Weissel at freshman orientation. Summer heat beamed onto the pavement outside the Samuel Riggs IV Alumni Center as I introduced myself to the two for the first time. We all had an interest in sport journalism.

It was naturally suggested that the three of us start a podcast talking about Maryland Athletics. We followed through. We recorded our first episode in the opening month of college.

The podcast had a simple format: interview a guest for the first 20-40 minutes, and then dicuss that week's games. The first guest was Tyler Cronin, Maryland baseball's trainer.

Flash forward one year later, we are now interviewing championship-winning coaches and legendary Maryland athletes. The show is now hosted on Testudo Times, with weekly hour-long episodes. My interviewing skills have substantially increased from the podcast.

Ralph Friedgen was Maryland’s head coach from 2001 to 2010, winning the Terps’ last conference championship in 2001 and finishing with a 75-50 record as a head coach. Friedgen was also named ACC Coach of the Year twice and was named the AP College Coach of the Year in 2001.  The former head coach discussed his unusual firing in 2010, how James Franklin almost became Maryland’s head coach and how he turned football around in College Park. Friedgen also revealed his relationship with Mike Locksley and where he thinks the program is headed.
